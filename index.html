The first video I ever uploaded to YouTube doesn't exist anymore, the channel doesn't even exist anymore.
IIRC it must have been in 2012 or 2013, and it was Playstation 2 gameplay captured by a mobile-phone camera or a digital camera, obviously that was no viral hit :D
Later on I tried my first steps with let's plays of an online airport manager and minecraft, which barely even hit 10fps on my laptop, so the recording was more like 1sfp (yes second per frame).

After (obviously) not having any success with these poor attempts I took a break until I created my current channel in 2015.
At launch it was called NaturNerd and exclusively focusing on Yu-Gi-Oh! replays of an online dueling platform.
These videos are privated nowadays, but I still have access to them and might show them one day :)
I kept uploading them in irregular intervals until late 2017, but it was the first time my content was positively acclaimed and that boosted my motivation ...

...to upload a couple of videos over the next 3 years, one of them - a csgo tournament prediction video - even hitting 892 clicks, personal record so far!

Then 2020 came around and that chance was well-used. I started let's playing again, but this time with a decent microphone and actual 1080p60 footage, but the time for let's plays was over, which I realized a couple of weeks in.
That is when I had my - so far - most successful idea: a miniseries, uploading a 20-30 second short-video of me opening a csgo case and giving away the low quality skins. And boy did that take off - 6 of them reached over 1000 views and a lot got close. This also prompted my channel to grow over 100 subscribers.
Simultaneously I tried my luck with some other formats, most of them focussing on csgo, they're all unlisted, but available in this playlist today.

Then on February 5th, 2022 I uploaded a video in which I explained why I decided to stop creating csgo related content and focus more on content evolving around myself.
Also I stopped having a fixed upload schedule, this lead to me being able to create higher quality videos, that are completely in line with the standard id like to uphold.

Thank you all for being a part of this journey, which is far from its end, I promise!
